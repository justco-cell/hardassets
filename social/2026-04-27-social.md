**Title:** Where Should I Actually Store My Stash? Home Safe vs Bank Box vs Vault — The Full Tradeoff Analysis

**Body:**

Just wrote a comprehensive breakdown of precious metals storage options because this question comes up constantly on the sub.

TL;DR:
- **70% home safe** = instant access, zero fees, your control. Tradeoff: theft and fire risk
- **25% bank safety deposit box** = bank-grade security, cheap ($50-200/yr), accessible during business hours. Tradeoff: can't access Friday night if SHTF
- **5% vault (optional)** = military-grade security, full insurance. Tradeoff: high fees ($100-300/yr), counterparty risk

The recommendation is *diversify* across all three. If your home burns down, 70% is there. If there's a bank seizure crisis, you've got 30% elsewhere. If premiums spike and you need to liquidate, 70% is instantly accessible.

The post also covers insurance specifics (most homeowner's policies cap metals at $2,500 unless you add a rider), theft prevention (bolt your safe, hide it, don't advertise), and why hidden storage is a bad idea for anything but emergency cash.

Most importantly: track it all in one dashboard. If you're split across home, bank, and vault, you need visibility into your total position.

## LinkedIn Post

**Headline:** Where Do You Actually Store Your Gold and Silver?

**Body:**

One of the biggest mistakes precious metals investors make is not planning storage before they buy.

A $50,000 metals position isn't just about the purchase decision — it's about security, insurance, access, and contingency planning.

Most investors default to one location (home safe). That's too concentrated.

Here's the smarter approach:
- 70% in home safe (instant access, zero fees, your control)
- 25% in bank safety deposit box (bank security, low cost, business-hour access)
- 5% in allocated vault (if large position, geographic diversification)

This split hedges three risks:
1) Home fire/theft → 30% survives elsewhere
2) Bank seizure in crisis → 70% still in your safe
3) Vault company failure → only small position affected

Plus the practical details most miss:
- Standard homeowner's insurance caps metals at $2,500. You need a rider for full coverage.
- Bank boxes have probate risk (heirs can't access immediately after your death).
- Vaults are expensive ($100-300/yr) but appropriate for very large positions.

If you're serious about hard assets, spend an hour planning storage before your first purchase. The cost of getting it wrong (lost metals, audit issues, insurance gaps) is much higher than diversifying across locations.
