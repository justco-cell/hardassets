I've been stacking precious metals for years but recently allocated 1% of my hard assets to Bitcoin (via an ETF). Here's my honest take:

**What Bitcoin does well:**
- Fixed supply: 21M coins mathematically enforced (unlike Fed printing)
- Infinite divisibility: Can own fractions instantly (gold requires melting)
- Portability: Private key on your phone (gold is 22 lbs per oz)
- Zero counterparty custodial risk if self-custodied
- Volatility is a feature if you're 10+ years out (forces rebalancing)

**What Gold still wins on:**
- 5,000 years of history vs 16 years of Bitcoin
- Lower volatility (3-5% weekly vs 5-10% daily for BTC)
- Institutional adoption (vaults, insurance, ETFs that work)
- No regulatory risk (Bitcoin could be restricted by governments)

**My allocation:**
5% gold + 2% silver + 1% Bitcoin = 8% total hard assets

If Bitcoin goes to $200k, my net worth improves. If it goes to zero, I lose 1% and still have my gold/silver core. It's hedging the hedge.

**Bitcoin and the hard asset investor: A macro framework**

The world watched Bitcoin rally to $78,400 this week while Morgan Stanley quietly positioned itself as the reserve manager for stablecoins. This isn't headlines—it's infrastructure.

For 15+ years, the hard asset conversation was simple: own gold and silver. Fixed supply. Inflation hedge. Store of value. All true.

But the 2024-2026 environment has forced a harder question: As central banks lose credibility and monetary debasement accelerates, should a diversified hard asset portfolio include a small Bitcoin allocation?

**The case is structural, not speculative:**

Central banks printed $8T in 2020-2025. M2 money supply up 40%. That's debasement. Bitcoin's supply is fixed at 21M coins by code, not promise. In a monetary crisis scenario, both precious metals and crypto serve the same function: they hold value when currency doesn't.

Institutional adoption has gone from 5% of investors (2020) to 25% (2026). Bitcoin ETFs pulled in $2B in eight days. This is no longer retail speculation.

**My framework for balanced allocation:**

For most investors: 8-12% in hard assets total. Split into:
- 6-7% precious metals (gold core, silver upside)
- 1-2% Bitcoin/crypto (tail-risk hedge, convexity bet)

It's not either/or. It's both. Gold for proven inflation protection. Bitcoin for tail-risk scenario where the monetary system breaks.

If you're allocating, dollar cost average over 6-12 months to smooth volatility. Rebalance quarterly when Bitcoin rallies 50%+. Track everything in one dashboard so you actually *see* your allocation drift.

The honest trade-off: Bitcoin's 10x-100x upside if the system breaks, vs 50-90% downside if adoption stalls. Gold's steady 3-5x in stagflation. Most of us should own both.

The middle ground is usually defensible.
