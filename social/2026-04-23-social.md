When geopolitical risk spikes, institutional investors don't debate it — they buy gold. Here's why.

Gold has one property no other asset has: it's not backed by any government. Bonds depend on government solvency. Equities depend on business stability and rule of law. When a ceasefire was announced on April 8, gold didn't crash — it repriced down from $4,793 to $4,746 as the premium compressed. Smart investors buy before the risk spike (premium low), sell after the ceasefire (premium compressed). Professional investors know this. You should too.
